RVDT Chassis

Typically RVDT's are included with systems that have engines equipped with electronic fuel control. The RVDT chassis is a 19" rack mount unit. The unit is universal and can operate at 85 VAC to 264 VAC at a frequency of 45 Hz to 65 Hz.

The RVDT chassis contains two independent digital to RVDT simulator cards. These cards convert a binary angle signal from the controller chassis to two linearly dependent voltages to be sent to the electronic engine control (EEC). The cards are powered by a ±15 VDC power supply.

Multiple chassis can be daisy chained (wired together in sequence) if different engines require different simulator card configurations. See Operation for instructions on how to daisy chain the chassis. The output of each chassis is connected only to the current engine on test’s EEC.

Two LED's on the back of the RVDT chassis turn green when the UECU-500 Mark III main power is turned on. These LED's provide status of the +15 VDC and -15 VDC power supplies inside the chassis.
