Throttle Controller

The throttle controller contains an Allen-Bradley ControlLogix PLC with discrete and analogue input/output modules, which controls the test engine based on inputs from the throttle quadrant. The controller chassis is mounted in a standard 19-inch rack. The unit is universal and can operate at 85 VAC to 264 VAC at a frequency of 45 Hz to 65 Hz.

The PLC runs ladder logic code to control the test engine through outputs to the actuators, resolvers and/or RVDT's as required. The throttle controller also interfaces with the various support systems to ensure safe and reliable control of the test engine:

  • proDAS
  • Facility Control System (FCS)
  • Engine Control System (ECS)
  • E-Stop
  • etc.

A power (ON/OFF) switch is located on the back of the throttle controller. A power receptacle and the following connectors are located on the back of the throttle controller:

  • J1 Quadrant
  • J2 Actuator
  • J3 Resolver A
  • J4 Resolver B
  • J5 PLC Comm
  • J6 Analog In
  • J7 Analog Out
  • J8 Discrete In
  • J9 Discrete Out

Two LED's on the back of the throttle controller turn green when the UECU-500 Mark III main power is turned on. These LED's provide status of the +5 VDC and +24 VDC power supplies inside the controller.
