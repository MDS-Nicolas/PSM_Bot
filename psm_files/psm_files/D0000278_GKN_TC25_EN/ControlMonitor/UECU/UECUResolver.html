Resolver Chassis

resolvers are typically included with systems that have engines equipped with electronic fuel control. The resolver chassis is a 19" rack mounted unit. The unit is universal and can operate at 85 VAC to 264 VAC at a frequency of 45 Hz to 65 Hz.

The resolver chassis contains two independent digital-to-resolver simulator cards. These cards convert a binary angle signal from the controller chassis to a sine and cosine voltage signal which is sent to the electronic engine control (EEC). The simulator cards are powered by a ±15 VDC power supply.

Multiple chassis can be daisy chained (wired together in sequence) if different engines require different simulator card configurations. See Operation for instructions on how to daisy chain the chassis.

Two LEDs on the back of the resolver chassis turn green when the UECU-500 Mark III main power is turned on. These LEDs provide status of the +15 VDC and -15 VDC power supplies inside the chassis.
