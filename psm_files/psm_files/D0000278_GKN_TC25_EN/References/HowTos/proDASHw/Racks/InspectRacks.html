How To – Inspect the Equipment Racks

This How To provides the steps required to inspect the equipment racks.

To Inspect the Equipment Racks

To avoid electric shock, disconnect power from the rack until the visual inspection and dusting are completed.

Electrostatic discharge can damage components and/or boards installed in the rack. To avoid damage from electrostatic discharge when handling components and/or boards, wear an electrostatic strap that is connected to a proper ground.

  1. Ensure that the racks and systems are properly powered-down. See How To – Power Down the Instrumentation for details.
  2. Open the rack enclosure.
  3. Ensure that the cooling fans are free of dust, rotating freely (no noise or binding) and produce adequate air flow. Use dry compressed air to blow any dust from the cooling fans.
  4. Using an anti-static vacuum, clean any filters or grates that are present.
  5. Perform a visual inspection and ensure that all components and cables are properly seated.
  6. Ensure that the cables do not contain any kinks or frayed sheathing.
  7. Inspect the top and sides of the racks. Remove any foreign objects or debris which could block air flow to the racks.
  8. Power up the racks and systems. See How To – Power Up the Instrumentation for details.
  9. Close the rack enclosure.
