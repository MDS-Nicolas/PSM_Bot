How To – Dust and Inspect the Internal Components on a Computer

This How To provides the steps required to dust and inspect the computer's internal components.

To Dust and Verify the Internal Components

To avoid electric shock, disconnect power from the computer until the dusting and visual inspection are completed. NOTE: The computer may also be connected to a UPS system.

Electrostatic discharge can damage components and/or boards on your computer system. To avoid damage from electrostatic discharge when handling components and/or boards, wear an electrostatic strap that is connected to a proper ground.

  1. Ensure that the computer system has been properly powered-down and that the power source has been disconnected.
  2. Disconnect any external cables from the computer and label their connection points.
  3. Open the computer enclosure.
  4. Using dry compressed air, blow any dust from the inside of the computer including the power supply.
  5. Using an anti-static vacuum, clean any filters or grates that are present on the computer system.
  6. Ensure that the cooling fans are free of dust, rotating freely (no noise or binding) and produce adequate air flow.
  7. Perform a visual inspection and ensure that all PCBs and internal cables are properly seated.
  8. Reinstall the computer case and reconnect any external cables.
  9. Ensure that the external cables are well seated and do not contain any kinks or frayed sheathing.
  10. Clean the computer's monitor with appropriate cleaning solution.
  11. Power-up the computer and ensure that it is operating properly.
