How To – Connect an Analogue Position Indicator or Data Acquisition System (DAS)

The analogue position outputs a +/- 10 VDC signal from a 15 bit digital to analogue converter. This signal is used for display of throttle lever position on DAS (MDS or customer supplied).

It requires a high impedance input (typically 100 kOhm or more) and should be shielded. The shield is ungrounded inside the controller chassis and should be connected at the customer end. A twisted pair plus shield cable is recommended.

When calibrating equipment is connected to the analogue output, be aware that the minimum and maximum output values are -180 Deg to +180 Deg. These positions are equal to -10 VDC to +10 VDC. These outputs are independent of the Lever Min and Lever Max values defined for the engine type.
