How To – Clean the Throttle Quadrant

This How To provides the steps required to clean the throttle quadrant.

The Throttle components are under power. To avoid personal injury or damage to equipment, disconnect power from the Throttle before working on the throttle quadrant, the controller or the actuator.

Static discharge may damage or degrade electronic components on a printed circuit board (PCB). Use a grounded wrist strap when handling printed circuit boards (PCB's). Store the PCB in an anti-static bag when the PCB is not being used.

Cleaning the Throttle Quadrant

  1. Power off the throttle chassis.
  2. Disconnect the throttle cable from the connector on the throttle quadrant.
  3. Remove the screws holding the throttle quadrant and remove from the console.
  4. Remove the screws holding the top of the throttle quadrant.
  5. Open the throttle quadrant.

    6. Do not use cleaning agents to clean the brushes or the frame.

  6. Use clean, contaminate-free compressed air to blow dust, loose fibres, and other debris from the brushes.

    7. Do not blow the dust, loose fibres, and other debris into the frame.

  7. Wipe the interior of the frame to remove dust, fibres, and debris.
  8. Close the throttle quadrant.
  9. Re-install the screws holding the top of the throttle quadrant.
  10. Reconnect the throttle cable to the connector on the throttle quadrant.
