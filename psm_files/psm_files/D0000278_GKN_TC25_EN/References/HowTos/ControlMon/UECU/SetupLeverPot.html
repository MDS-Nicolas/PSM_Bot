How To – Set Up the Lever Potentiometer

This How To provides the steps required to set up the lever potentiometer.

The Throttle components are under power. To avoid personal injury or damage to equipment, disconnect power from the Throttle before working on the throttle quadrant, the controller or the actuator.

Static discharge may damage or degrade electronic components on a printed circuit board (PCB). Use a grounded wrist strap when handling printed circuit boards (PCB's). Store the PCB in an anti-static bag when the PCB is not being used.

To Set Up the Lever Potentiometer

  1. Power off the throttle chassis.
  2. Disconnect the throttle cable from the connector on the throttle quadrant.
  3. Remove the screws holding the throttle quadrant to the console.
  4. Remove the throttle quadrant from the console.
  5. Remove the screws holding the top of the throttle quadrant.
  6. Open the throttle quadrant.
  7. Loosen the set screw on the potentiometer coupler with an Allen wrench.
  8. Move the lever to the centre point of its travel.
  9. Connect a voltmeter to the pot (on the Ohm setting) with the negative probe on one end of the pot and the positive probe on the potentiometer centre tap.
  10. Rotate the pot until the resistance reads approximately 1600 Ohm (+/- 25 Ohm). Ensure the lever doesn’t move.
  11. Tighten the set screw in the coupler.
  12. Move the lever through out its entire length of travel to ensure that the potentiometer does not hit one of its internal stops.
  13. Record the resistances measured at either end of the lever travel. The resistances should be approximately 800 Ohm and 2250 Ohm (+/- 25 Ohm).
  14. Disconnect the voltmeter.
  15. Close the throttle quadrant.
  16. Re-install the screws holding the top of the throttle quadrant.
  17. Re-install the throttle quadrant in the console.
  18. Re-install the screws holding the throttle quadrant to the console.
  19. Re-attach the throttle cable to the connector on the throttle quadrant.
  20. Turn on power to the throttle chassis.
  21. Perform a lever calibration (see How To for procedure). Use the full length of travel as the MIN and MAX calibration points.
