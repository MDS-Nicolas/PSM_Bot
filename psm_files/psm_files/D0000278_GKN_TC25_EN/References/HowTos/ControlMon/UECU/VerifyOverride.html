How To – Verify the Actuator Override System Operation

This How To provides the steps required to verify the actuator override system operation.

The Throttle components are under power. To avoid personal injury or damage to equipment, disconnect power from the Throttle before working on the throttle quadrant, the controller or the actuator.

Static discharge may damage or degrade electronic components on a printed circuit board (PCB). Use a grounded wrist strap when handling printed circuit boards (PCB's). Store the PCB in an anti-static bag when the PCB is not being used.

To Verify the Override System Operation

The override system should be tested periodically.

  1. Ensure that it is safe to operate the override system (e.g. personnel are not working on or near the actuator).
    If possible, disconnect all linkages and equipment from the actuator output shaft. If not possible to do so, then ensure it is safe to operate the actuator and all equipment connected to the actuator lever arm.
  2. Using the Throttle control system, drive the actuator to 100% (full Throttle).
  3. Press the Actuator Override button.
  4. Verify that the actuator has returned to the pre-set override position (typically idle).
  5. Re-set the override system as per the procedure “Re-setting the Throttle Override System”.
  6. Re-connect any linkages and equipment to the actuator output shaft.
