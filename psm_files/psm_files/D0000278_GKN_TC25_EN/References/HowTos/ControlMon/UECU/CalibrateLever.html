How To – Calibrate the Throttle Lever

This How To provides the steps required to calibrate the throttle lever.

The Throttle components are under power. To avoid personal injury or damage to equipment, disconnect power from the Throttle before working on the throttle quadrant, the controller or the actuator.

Static discharge may damage or degrade electronic components on a printed circuit board (PCB). Use a grounded wrist strap when handling printed circuit boards (PCB's). Store the PCB in an anti-static bag when the PCB is not being used.

Calibrating the Lever

The lever travel can be calibrated from the throttle quadrant display.

The user is prompted to move the lever to the minimum travel position and then the maximum travel position. The throttle lever has a potentiometer to provide position feedback to the controller. This signal is calibrated such that full lever travel is defined as a percentage. This enables the software to redefine the amount of lever travel on a per engine basis without the need to recalibrate the lever.

The lever should be recalibrated on a yearly basis.

The lever calibration data is saved in the Channel Calibration Data Configuration file (calibrationData.xml).

For details, see How To – Calibrate a Channel.
