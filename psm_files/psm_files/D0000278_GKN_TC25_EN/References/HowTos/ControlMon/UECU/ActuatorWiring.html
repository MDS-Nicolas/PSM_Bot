How To – Check the Actuator Enclosure Assembly

This How To provides the steps required to check the screws, fasteners, and wiring in the Actuator Enclosure Assembly.

The throttle components are under power. To avoid personal injury or damage to equipment, disconnect power from the throttle before working on the throttle quadrant, the controller or the actuator.

Static discharge may damage or degrade electronic components on a printed circuit board (PCB). Use a grounded wrist strap when handling printed circuit boards (PCB's). Store the PCB in an anti-static bag when the PCB is not being used.

To Check the Screws, Fasteners, and Wiring in the Actuator Enclosure Assembly

  1. Turn off air to the override pneumatic system.
  2. Disconnect air lines and electrical connectors.
  3. Disconnect all linkages and equipment from the actuator output shaft.
  4. At the actuator enclosure assembly, remove the screws holding the cover to the bottom plate and the three vertical plates of the actuator enclosure assembly and remove the cover.

    5. To avoid damaging the printed circuit boards (PCB), do not over-tighten the screws holding the PCB's to the base plate.

  5. Check all screws and fasteners in the actuator enclosure and tighten the screws and fasteners as necessary. Do not over-tighten the screws.
  6. Check all wiring for any sign of overheating such as discoloured insulation.
  7. Check all wiring for any sign of cracking or deterioration of the insulation.
  8. Check the integrity of all solder joints.
  9. Repair and/or replace all damaged wiring and solder joints.
  10. Make sure the cause of the damage has been isolated and repaired.
  11. Prior to re-assembly, replace the weather stripping.
  12. Position the cover on the bottom and vertical plates of the actuator enclosure assembly and, using the screws, secure the cover to the actuator enclosure assembly.
  13. Re-connect all linkages and equipment to the actuator output shaft.
  14. Re-connect the air lines and electrical connectors.
  15. Turn on the air to the override pneumatic system.
