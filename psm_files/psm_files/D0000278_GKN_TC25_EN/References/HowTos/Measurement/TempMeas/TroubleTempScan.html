How To – Troubleshoot Trigger Problems with DTS3250/DTS4050 Temperature Scanners

This How To provides the steps required to troubleshoot trigger problems with temperature scanners.

Temperature scanners use either hardware or software triggers to control the flow of data to the Host Computer. MDS usually uses a hardware trigger to ensure that the Host Computer receives a snapshot of data sent at the same time.

Trigger problems with temperature scanners result in a loss of communications between the scanners and proDAS. The most common time for this error to occur is during the Start Scan process. A trigger problem is indicated if the Unified Event Log (UEL) shows a loss of communication message for all scanners.

Scanners using hardware triggers may be configured to use software triggering as a temporary solution to the problem. However, software triggering does not ensure time synchronization of the data.

To Troubleshoot Trigger Problems with Temperature Scanners

To prevent injury and damage to equipment, the operation and or maintenance should be carried out by a qualified technician. Observe the manufacturer's maintenance and repair guidelines and precautions.

  1. Verify the power supply to the trigger circuit as shown on drawings:
    • A-0000277, Assembly, Wiring, GKN, Test Cell 25, Scanner Interfaces
  2. Verify the trigger 12V LED light is on in Equipment Rack CM4 A1. If the LED light is off, make sure the breaker switch next to the power supply is on.
  3. Ensure the DIO channel for the DTS3250/DTS4050 trigger is configured correctly.
    • In the Channel Editor, confirm that the calculated trigger channel is defined with the same acquisition rate as the GASSDIO trigger channel.
    • Confirm that the equation for the calculated trigger channel causes it to change state. For example: DTS3250Trigger_Ref=! DTS3250Trigger_Ref

    The calculated trigger channel must have a different name than the GASSDIO trigger channel.

    • In the Channel Editor, confirm that the GASSDIO trigger channel is defined with the proper acquisition rate and set the channel to an output channel. The output channel references the calculated trigger channel (e.g. ‘DTS3250Trigger_Ref’)

    The proper acquisition rate for the GASSDIO trigger channel is normally 2 times the required scanner scan rate. For example, if the scan rate for the scanner is 100 samples a second, define the acquisition rate for the trigger channel as 200 samples a second. This is because each time the channel is scanned, it toggles ON and OFF and therefore its acquisition rate must be at least two times the scan rate, as the channel must toggle ON and OFF in order to complete one trigger cycle.
