VXI Hardware Interface

Overview

The VME Extended for Instrumentation (VXI) Hardware Interfaces convert analogue signals from engine or facility transducers into digital form for the Linux Host Computer using Signal Conditioning Plug-On (SCP) cards installed on an Analogue to Digital (A/D) conversion card.

Each SCP scans and digitises the analogue signals, and stores them in the on-board memory of the A/D card. The GASSVXI Subsystem within proDAS scans and extracts the data as a block from the cards and stores the values in the proDAS Current Value Table (CVT). The SCP's are dedicated to processing and transmitting different measurement signals, including:

  • general analogue signals
  • general frequency signals
  • thermocouple
  • pneumatic pressure
  • liquid pressure
  • mechanical measurement
  • fuel density measurement
  • temperature
  • rotational speed
  • fuel flow
  • environmental measurements

Analogue and Frequency Input signals are configured as GASSVXI Subsystem channels.

Analogue Output signals are configured as GASSAO.

Digital Input/Output signals are configured as GASSDIO.

The VXI System:

  • accepts analogue voltage inputs
  • conditions the analogue voltage inputs (gain and filter)
  • accepts frequency measurements
  • performs analogue-to-digital conversion of the analogue voltage and frequency inputs for use by proDAS
  • outputs voltage or current
  • accepts discrete input signals from switches or sensors
  • controls discrete devices such as relays or solenoids

In case of a card failure, the VXI System continues to operate normally with the remaining cards.
