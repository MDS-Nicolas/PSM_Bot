Pneumatic Pressure Measurement

Operation

The Pneumatic Pressure Measurement System measures the following pressures:

  • engine and facility air pressures
    • engine channels:
      • 64 channels (-12 to +30 psi)
      • 32 channels (0 to +100 psi)
  • barometric pressure (client-supplied)

The system is ready for operation if no error messages appear in the Unified Event Log (UEL). Pressure scanning starts after proDAS is configured and scanning, and has started a synchronization pulse for data transfer. System measurements are displayed on a Real-Time Display Computer (RTD). The engineering unit (EU) conversion is performed by the scanner. These values can be converted by proDAS to absolute measurements by adding the ambient pressure measurement to each channel.

The pressure scanners transmit data to proDAS through an independent Ethernet connection.

Pressure Scanner Purging, Zeroing and Calibrating

The Pressure Scanner Functions Panel on the proDAS Management GUI Computer controls the purging and zeroing functions of scanners. The pressure scanners should be purged and zeroed before each engine test.

Supply pressure must be between 80 to 120 PSI to ensure proper operation of scanners, when switching between purging, zeroing and measurement modes. Supply and purge air for the scanners is provided by client-supplied auxiliary air system.

For procedures on zeroing and purging pressure scanners, see the How To procedures.

Calibration

The 9216 pressure scanner channels are defined in the Pressure Brick Subsystem (PBS), the test cell depression pressure channels are defined in the GASSVXI Subsystem and the ambient barometric pressure channels are defined in the Ambient Temp/Humidity (ATH) Subsystem.

Pneumatic Pressure Measurement channels are configured with the Channel Editor. Individual sensors for the test cell depression and ambient barometric pressure channels are configured with the Sensor Editor.

The manufacturer recommends calibrating the 9216 scanners every six months. The calibration information and the calibration due date is stored in the individual transducers' Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory (EEPROM). A dialogue box is displayed on a Real-Time Display screen that warns of an upcoming due date (within 30 days) when proDAS is configured. The scanners must be re-calibrated by a certified calibration facility.

Remove any scanner that requires calibration from service until it has been re-calibrated.
